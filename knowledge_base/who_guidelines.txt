WHO Healthy Diet Guidelines
===========================

General Principles:
- A healthy diet helps protect against malnutrition in all its forms, as well as noncommunicable diseases (NCDs), including diabetes, heart disease, stroke, and cancer.
- Limit intake of free sugars to less than 10% of total energy intake. A further reduction to less than 5% (about 25g or 6 teaspoons per day) provides additional health benefits.
- Keep salt intake to less than 5g per day (equivalent to sodium intake of less than 2g per day) to help prevent hypertension and reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke.
- Ensure iodized salt is used.

Fats Guidelines:
- Limit total fat intake to less than 30% of total energy intake.
- Shift fat consumption away from saturated fats (found in fatty meat, butter, palm and coconut oil, cream, cheese, ghee, and lard) and trans-fats to unsaturated fats (found in fish, avocado, and nuts, and in sunflower, soybean, canola, and olive oils).
- Eliminate industrial trans-fats (found in baked and fried foods, pre-packaged snacks, and cooking oils).

Fruits and Vegetables:
- Eat at least 400g (i.e. five portions) of fruit and vegetables per day, excluding potatoes, sweet potatoes, cassava, and other starchy roots.
- Eating fruit and vegetables lowers the risk of cardiovascular diseases and certain cancers.

Water Intake:
- Drink at least 2.0 to 2.5 liters of clean water daily to sustain metabolic activities and ensure hydration.
- Active individuals or those in warm climates should increase water intake appropriately.
